## Problem Definition

**Setup**: A single quadrotor executes a timed sequence of four aerobatic manoeuvres at a safe
altitude of $z_0 = 10$ m. The manoeuvres are performed back-to-back with a 2-second stabilisation
pause between each:

1. **Backflip** — 360° rotation about the body $y$-axis (pitch) over $t_{flip} = 1.5$ s.
2. **Barrel Roll** — 360° rotation about the body $x$-axis (roll) over $t_{roll} = 1.5$ s.
3. **Immelmann Turn** — 180° half-loop (pitch up) followed immediately by a 180° roll to restore
   upright attitude, executed over $t_{imm} = 3.0$ s.
4. **Inverted Hover** — the drone maintains $R = \mathrm{diag}(1, -1, -1)$ (rotated 180° about the
   $x$-axis, thrust pointing down) for $t_{inv} = 2.0$ s; thrust is doubled to compensate gravity.

Each manoeuvre is parameterised as a smooth time-varying attitude reference $R_{ref}(t) \in SO(3)$,
generated by slerp-interpolating between attitude waypoints on the rotation-matrix manifold. A
**geometric controller on SO(3)** tracks $R_{ref}(t)$ at all attitudes without any small-angle
approximation, while a separate **position PD controller** drives the drone back to its home
position $(0, 0, z_0)$ after each manoeuvre.

**Roles**:
- **Drone**: single quadrotor, mass $m = 0.5$ kg, inertia tensor $I = \mathrm{diag}(I_{xx}, I_{yy},
  I_{zz})$; full 6-DOF rigid-body dynamics; four-rotor thrust allocation model.
- **Attitude reference**: smooth $SO(3)$ trajectory generated by geodesic interpolation (slerp on
  rotation matrices via quaternion intermediate representation).
- **Geometric controller**: computes angular velocity error and attitude error purely in terms of
  rotation matrices — valid at any attitude including inverted flight.
- **Position controller**: outer-loop PD that produces a desired total thrust direction; projects
  onto the current body $z$-axis.

**Objective**: Execute all four manoeuvres in sequence while satisfying:

1. **Attitude tracking**: RMS geometric attitude error $\bar{e}_R \leq 0.15$ rad throughout each
   manoeuvre.
2. **Angular rate bound**: peak angular rate $\|\Omega\|_\infty \leq \Omega_{max} = 12$ rad/s.
3. **Manoeuvre completion**: each manoeuvre reaches its target rotation within $\pm 5°$ of the
   intended final attitude before the stabilisation pause ends.
4. **Position recovery**: after each manoeuvre the drone returns to within 0.5 m of the home
   position.

**Comparison strategies**:

1. **Euler-angle PID (baseline)** — attitude tracked using roll/pitch/yaw PID on Euler angles;
   breaks down near $\pm 90°$ pitch/roll due to gimbal lock and linearisation assumptions.
2. **Quaternion PD** — attitude error expressed as a quaternion error $\tilde{q}$; avoids gimbal
   lock but has double-cover ambiguity that can cause unwinding.
3. **Geometric control on SO(3) (proposed)** — attitude error computed directly as a skew-symmetric
   matrix on $SO(3)$; globally valid, no singularities, no unwinding.

## Mathematical Model

### Full Quadrotor Dynamics

The drone is a rigid body in $SE(3)$. Let $p \in \mathbb{R}^3$ be the position of the centre of
mass, $v = \dot{p}$ the velocity, $R \in SO(3)$ the rotation matrix from body to world frame, and
$\Omega \in \mathbb{R}^3$ the angular velocity expressed in the body frame. The equations of motion
are:

$$\dot{p} = v$$

$$m\dot{v} = f\,R\mathbf{e}_3 - mg\,\mathbf{e}_3$$

$$\dot{R} = R\,\hat{\Omega}$$

$$I\dot{\Omega} = \tau - \Omega \times I\Omega$$

where $f \geq 0$ is the scalar total thrust, $\tau \in \mathbb{R}^3$ is the net torque in the body
frame, $\mathbf{e}_3 = (0,0,1)^\top$, $g = 9.81$ m/s², and $\hat{\cdot}: \mathbb{R}^3 \to
\mathfrak{so}(3)$ is the hat (skew-symmetric) map:

$$\hat{\Omega} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -\Omega_z & \Omega_y \\ \Omega_z & 0 & -\Omega_x \\ -\Omega_y & \Omega_x & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$

### Vee Map and Attitude Error on $SO(3)$

The **vee map** $(\cdot)^\vee : \mathfrak{so}(3) \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is the inverse of the hat map.
For any skew-symmetric matrix $S \in \mathfrak{so}(3)$:

$$S^\vee = \begin{pmatrix} S_{32} \\ S_{13} \\ S_{21} \end{pmatrix}$$

The geometric attitude error between the current rotation $R$ and the reference $R_{ref}$ is:

$$e_R = \frac{1}{2}\!\left(R_{ref}^\top R - R^\top R_{ref}\right)^\vee \in \mathbb{R}^3$$

This error vector lives in $\mathbb{R}^3$, is zero if and only if $R = R_{ref}$, and is well-defined
at all attitudes (no singularity, no gimbal lock). The corresponding angular velocity error in the
body frame is:

$$e_\Omega = \Omega - R^\top R_{ref}\,\Omega_{ref} \in \mathbb{R}^3$$

where $\Omega_{ref} = ({\dot{R}_{ref}}^\top R_{ref})^\vee$ is the reference angular velocity in the
reference body frame.

### Geometric Attitude Controller

The control torque is (Lee, Leok, McClamroch 2010):

$$\tau = -K_R\,e_R - K_\Omega\,e_\Omega + \Omega \times I\Omega - I\!\left(\hat{\Omega}\,R^\top R_{ref}\,\Omega_{ref} - R^\top R_{ref}\,\dot{\Omega}_{ref}\right)$$

where $K_R, K_\Omega \in \mathbb{R}^{3\times 3}$ are symmetric positive-definite gain matrices
(diagonal in practice). The feedforward term $\Omega \times I\Omega$ cancels the gyroscopic moment;
the remaining feedforward cancels the centripetal and reference angular-acceleration contributions,
improving tracking during fast manoeuvres.

### Position Controller and Thrust

The desired acceleration from a PD position controller is:

$$a_{des} = -K_x\,e_x - K_v\,e_v + g\,\mathbf{e}_3 + \ddot{p}_{ref}$$

where $e_x = p - p_{ref}$ and $e_v = v - v_{ref}$. The desired thrust magnitude is obtained by
projecting $a_{des}$ onto the current body $z$-axis:

$$f = m\,a_{des} \cdot R\mathbf{e}_3$$

This expression is valid at all attitudes: during inverted hover ($R\mathbf{e}_3 \approx
-\mathbf{e}_3$) the required thrust is negative in the world-frame sense, but the rotor command
is $f > 0$ because the body $z$-axis now points downward.

### Attitude Reference Generation on $SO(3)$

Each manoeuvre is defined by an initial rotation $R_0 \in SO(3)$ and a target rotation $R_f \in
SO(3)$. The smooth reference trajectory is obtained via geodesic (slerp) interpolation. Converting
to unit quaternions $q_0, q_f$ and using a cubic-Hermite blend parameter $s(t)$:

$$s(t) = 3\!\left(\frac{t - t_s}{t_e - t_s}\right)^2 - 2\!\left(\frac{t - t_s}{t_e - t_s}\right)^3, \qquad t \in [t_s, t_e]$$

$$q_{ref}(t) = \mathrm{slerp}(q_0,\, q_f,\, s(t))$$

$$R_{ref}(t) = \mathrm{quat\_to\_matrix}(q_{ref}(t))$$

The cubic-Hermite blend ensures zero angular velocity at the start and end of each manoeuvre
segment (smooth boundary conditions), which reduces peak torque demand and improves tracking.

The reference angular velocity is computed by finite differences at each timestep:

$$\Omega_{ref}(t) \approx \left(\dot{R}_{ref}^\top R_{ref}\right)^\vee = \frac{1}{\Delta t}\!\left(R_{ref}(t+\Delta t)^\top R_{ref}(t) - I\right)^\vee$$

### Rotor Thrust Allocation

The scalar thrust $f$ and body torque $\tau$ are mapped to four rotor speeds
$\omega_i$ (rad/s) via the allocation matrix $\Gamma$:

$$\begin{pmatrix} f \\ \tau_x \\ \tau_y \\ \tau_z \end{pmatrix} = \Gamma \begin{pmatrix} \omega_1^2 \\ \omega_2^2 \\ \omega_3^2 \\ \omega_4^2 \end{pmatrix}, \qquad \Gamma = k_f \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\ -l & l & l & -l \\ l & -l & l & -l \\ -k_m & -k_m & k_m & k_m \end{pmatrix}$$

where $k_f$ is the thrust coefficient, $l$ the arm length, and $k_m$ the torque-to-thrust ratio.
Rotor speeds are clipped to $[\omega_{min}, \omega_{max}]$ before integration.

### Performance Metrics

**Geometric attitude tracking error** (integrated over manoeuvre $k$):

$$\bar{e}_{R,k} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{t_{e,k} - t_{s,k}} \int_{t_{s,k}}^{t_{e,k}} \|e_R(t)\|^2\, dt}$$

**Peak angular rate**:

$$\Omega_{peak} = \max_{t} \|\Omega(t)\|_2$$

**Manoeuvre completion angle** (residual attitude error at manoeuvre end):

$$\delta_k = \arccos\!\left(\frac{\mathrm{tr}(R_{ref}(t_{e,k})^\top R(t_{e,k})) - 1}{2}\right) \cdot \frac{180}{\pi} \quad [\text{degrees}]$$

This is the geodesic distance on $SO(3)$ converted to degrees; $\delta_k \leq 5°$ indicates
successful completion.

**Position recovery error**:

$$\epsilon_{pos,k} = \|p(t_{s,k+1}) - p_{home}\|$$

at the start of the next manoeuvre (after the stabilisation pause).

```python
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from mpl_toolkits.mplot3d import Axes3D
from matplotlib.animation import FuncAnimation
from scipy.spatial.transform import Rotation, Slerp

# ── Physical constants ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
G           = 9.81          # m/s²
MASS        = 0.5           # kg
I_DIAG      = np.array([4.9e-3, 4.9e-3, 8.8e-3])   # kg·m²  (Crazyflie-like)
I_MAT       = np.diag(I_DIAG)
I_INV       = np.diag(1.0 / I_DIAG)

# ── Rotor allocation ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ARM_LEN     = 0.17          # m   arm length
KF          = 3.16e-10      # N·s²  thrust coefficient (per (rad/s)^2)
KM          = 7.94e-12      # N·m·s²  torque coefficient
OMEGA_MIN   = 0.0           # rad/s
OMEGA_MAX   = 2000.0        # rad/s  (upper physical limit)

ALLOC = KF * np.array([
    [ 1,      1,      1,      1     ],   # thrust
    [-ARM_LEN, ARM_LEN, ARM_LEN, -ARM_LEN],  # tau_x
    [ ARM_LEN, -ARM_LEN, ARM_LEN, -ARM_LEN], # tau_y
    [-KM/KF,  -KM/KF,  KM/KF,  KM/KF ],   # tau_z
])
ALLOC_INV = np.linalg.pinv(ALLOC)

# ── Controller gains ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KR          = np.diag([6.0, 6.0, 3.0])     # attitude error gain
KW          = np.diag([0.5, 0.5, 0.3])     # angular velocity error gain
KX          = 4.0                           # position error gain (scalar * I)
KV          = 3.0                           # velocity error gain (scalar * I)

# ── Simulation ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DT          = 0.005         # s  integration timestep
Z_HOME      = 10.0          # m  cruise altitude
HOME_POS    = np.array([0.0, 0.0, Z_HOME])

# ── Manoeuvre schedule ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Each entry: (name, R_start_euler_deg [roll,pitch,yaw], R_end_euler_deg, duration_s)
# All rotations relative to the level upright hover attitude (identity).
MANOEUVRE_SCHEDULE = [
    ('hover_init',     [0,   0, 0], [  0,    0, 0], 2.0),   # initial hover
    ('backflip',       [0,   0, 0], [  0,  360, 0], 1.5),   # 360° pitch
    ('stabilise_1',    [0, 360, 0], [  0,    0, 0], 2.0),   # recover upright
    ('barrel_roll',    [0,   0, 0], [360,    0, 0], 1.5),   # 360° roll
    ('stabilise_2',  [360,   0, 0], [  0,    0, 0], 2.0),   # recover
    ('immelmann_half', [0,   0, 0], [  0,  180, 0], 1.5),   # half loop (nose up)
    ('immelmann_roll', [0, 180, 0], [180,  180, 0], 1.5),   # half roll → upright
    ('stabilise_3',  [180, 180, 0], [  0,    0, 0], 2.0),   # recover
    ('inverted_hover', [0,   0, 0], [180,    0, 0], 0.5),   # flip to inverted
    ('inverted_hold',  [180, 0, 0], [180,    0, 0], 2.0),   # hold inverted
    ('recover',      [180,   0, 0], [  0,    0, 0], 1.5),   # flip back
    ('final_hover',    [0,   0, 0], [  0,    0, 0], 2.0),   # final stabilise
]


def euler_to_R(angles_deg):
    """Convert [roll, pitch, yaw] in degrees to 3x3 rotation matrix (ZYX convention)."""
    return Rotation.from_euler('xyz', angles_deg, degrees=True).as_matrix()


def R_to_quat(R):
    """Rotation matrix to scipy Rotation object."""
    return Rotation.from_matrix(R)


def vee(S):
    """Vee map: extract the 3-vector from a 3×3 skew-symmetric matrix."""
    return np.array([S[2, 1], S[0, 2], S[1, 0]])


def hat(v):
    """Hat map: 3-vector to 3×3 skew-symmetric matrix."""
    return np.array([
        [    0, -v[2],  v[1]],
        [ v[2],     0, -v[0]],
        [-v[1],  v[0],     0],
    ])


def attitude_error(R, R_ref):
    """Geometric attitude error e_R on SO(3)."""
    return 0.5 * vee(R_ref.T @ R - R.T @ R_ref)


def build_reference_trajectory():
    """
    Build the full time-parameterised attitude reference (R_ref, Omega_ref) and
    position reference (p_ref, v_ref, a_ref) for all manoeuvres.
    Returns arrays indexed by timestep.
    """
    # Count total timesteps
    total_time = sum(seg[3] for seg in MANOEUVRE_SCHEDULE)
    n_steps    = int(total_time / DT) + 1

    R_ref_arr    = np.zeros((n_steps, 3, 3))
    Omega_ref_arr = np.zeros((n_steps, 3))
    p_ref_arr    = np.tile(HOME_POS, (n_steps, 1))  # position stays at home
    v_ref_arr    = np.zeros((n_steps, 3))
    seg_id_arr   = np.empty(n_steps, dtype=object)

    step  = 0
    for seg_name, r_start_deg, r_end_deg, dur in MANOEUVRE_SCHEDULE:
        n_seg = int(dur / DT)
        R0 = euler_to_R(r_start_deg)
        Rf = euler_to_R(r_end_deg)

        # Slerp via scipy Rotation
        key_rots  = Rotation.concatenate([Rotation.from_matrix(R0),
                                          Rotation.from_matrix(Rf)])
        key_times = [0.0, 1.0]
        slerp_fn  = Slerp(key_times, key_rots)

        for k in range(n_seg):
            if step >= n_steps:
                break
            # Cubic-Hermite blend parameter
            frac = k / max(n_seg - 1, 1)
            s    = 3 * frac**2 - 2 * frac**3   # smooth step

            R_now = slerp_fn(s).as_matrix()
            R_ref_arr[step] = R_now
            seg_id_arr[step] = seg_name

            # Finite-difference reference angular velocity (look-ahead)
            if k < n_seg - 1:
                s_next  = 3 * ((k+1)/max(n_seg-1,1))**2 - 2 * ((k+1)/max(n_seg-1,1))**3
                R_next  = slerp_fn(min(s_next, 1.0)).as_matrix()
                dR      = R_next.T @ R_now - np.eye(3)
                Omega_ref_arr[step] = vee(dR) / DT
            else:
                Omega_ref_arr[step] = np.zeros(3)

            step += 1

    # Fill any remaining steps (rounding) with last values
    for k in range(step, n_steps):
        R_ref_arr[k]     = R_ref_arr[step - 1]
        Omega_ref_arr[k] = np.zeros(3)
        seg_id_arr[k]    = seg_id_arr[step - 1]

    return R_ref_arr, Omega_ref_arr, p_ref_arr, v_ref_arr, seg_id_arr, n_steps


def geometric_control(R, Omega, R_ref, Omega_ref, Omega_ref_dot,
                      p, v, p_ref, v_ref, a_ref):
    """
    Compute total thrust f and body torque tau using geometric control on SO(3).
    Returns (f, tau).
    """
    # ── Attitude control ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    e_R = attitude_error(R, R_ref)
    e_W = Omega - R.T @ R_ref @ Omega_ref

    # Feedforward: cancel gyroscopic + centripetal reference terms
    ff = (np.cross(Omega, I_MAT @ Omega)
          - I_MAT @ (hat(Omega) @ R.T @ R_ref @ Omega_ref
                     - R.T @ R_ref @ Omega_ref_dot))

    tau = -KR @ e_R - KW @ e_W + ff

    # ── Position / thrust control ─────────────────────────────────────────────
    e_x = p - p_ref
    e_v = v - v_ref
    a_des = -KX * e_x - KV * e_v + G * np.array([0, 0, 1]) + a_ref

    # Project desired acceleration onto body z-axis (current thrust direction)
    b3 = R[:, 2]
    f  = MASS * np.dot(a_des, b3)

    return f, tau


def rotor_commands(f, tau):
    """Convert (f, tau) to four squared rotor speeds; clip to valid range."""
    wrench  = np.array([f, tau[0], tau[1], tau[2]])
    omega_sq = ALLOC_INV @ wrench
    omega_sq = np.clip(omega_sq, OMEGA_MIN**2, OMEGA_MAX**2)
    return omega_sq


def simulate(strategy='geometric'):
    """
    Simulate the aerobatic sequence.
    strategy: 'geometric' | 'euler_pid' (simplified comparison)
    Returns log dict.
    """
    (R_ref_arr, Omega_ref_arr, p_ref_arr,
     v_ref_arr, seg_id_arr, n_steps) = build_reference_trajectory()

    # State initialisation
    R    = np.eye(3)
    Omega = np.zeros(3)
    p    = HOME_POS.copy()
    v    = np.zeros(3)

    log = {
        't':       [],
        'p':       [],
        'v':       [],
        'R':       [],
        'Omega':   [],
        'e_R':     [],
        'f':       [],
        'tau':     [],
        'seg':     [],
        'R_ref':   [],
    }

    for k in range(n_steps):
        t = k * DT

        R_ref     = R_ref_arr[k]
        Omega_ref = Omega_ref_arr[k]
        Omega_ref_dot = (Omega_ref_arr[min(k+1, n_steps-1)] - Omega_ref) / DT

        p_ref = p_ref_arr[k]
        v_ref = v_ref_arr[k]
        a_ref = np.zeros(3)

        if strategy == 'geometric':
            f, tau = geometric_control(R, Omega, R_ref, Omega_ref, Omega_ref_dot,
                                       p, v, p_ref, v_ref, a_ref)
        else:
            # Euler-angle PID (simplified): ignore full attitude, use small-angle approx
            euler = Rotation.from_matrix(R).as_euler('xyz', degrees=False)
            euler_ref = Rotation.from_matrix(R_ref).as_euler('xyz', degrees=False)
            e_euler = euler - euler_ref
            tau = -np.array([8.0, 8.0, 3.0]) * e_euler - np.array([0.5, 0.5, 0.3]) * Omega
            e_x = p - p_ref
            e_v = v - v_ref
            a_des = -KX * e_x - KV * e_v + G * np.array([0, 0, 1])
            f = MASS * np.dot(a_des, R[:, 2])

        # Saturate total thrust
        f = np.clip(f, 0.0, 4 * KF * OMEGA_MAX**2)

        # Logging
        e_R = attitude_error(R, R_ref)
        log['t'].append(t)
        log['p'].append(p.copy())
        log['v'].append(v.copy())
        log['R'].append(R.copy())
        log['Omega'].append(Omega.copy())
        log['e_R'].append(e_R.copy())
        log['f'].append(float(f))
        log['tau'].append(tau.copy())
        log['seg'].append(seg_id_arr[k])
        log['R_ref'].append(R_ref.copy())

        # ── Dynamics integration (RK4) ─────────────────────────────────────────
        def derivatives(p_, v_, R_, Omega_, f_, tau_):
            dp     = v_
            dv     = (f_ * R_[:, 2] / MASS) - G * np.array([0, 0, 1])
            dR     = R_ @ hat(Omega_)
            dOmega = I_INV @ (tau_ - np.cross(Omega_, I_MAT @ Omega_))
            return dp, dv, dR, dOmega

        dp1, dv1, dR1, dO1 = derivatives(p, v, R, Omega, f, tau)
        dp2, dv2, dR2, dO2 = derivatives(p + 0.5*DT*dp1, v + 0.5*DT*dv1,
                                          R + 0.5*DT*dR1, Omega + 0.5*DT*dO1, f, tau)
        dp3, dv3, dR3, dO3 = derivatives(p + 0.5*DT*dp2, v + 0.5*DT*dv2,
                                          R + 0.5*DT*dR2, Omega + 0.5*DT*dO2, f, tau)
        dp4, dv4, dR4, dO4 = derivatives(p + DT*dp3, v + DT*dv3,
                                          R + DT*dR3, Omega + DT*dO3, f, tau)

        p     += DT/6 * (dp1 + 2*dp2 + 2*dp3 + dp4)
        v     += DT/6 * (dv1 + 2*dv2 + 2*dv3 + dv4)
        R     += DT/6 * (dR1 + 2*dR2 + 2*dR3 + dR4)
        Omega += DT/6 * (dO1 + 2*dO2 + 2*dO3 + dO4)

        # Re-orthonormalise R (Gram–Schmidt, keeps SO(3) manifold)
        U, _, Vt = np.linalg.svd(R)
        R = U @ Vt
        if np.linalg.det(R) < 0:
            R[:, -1] *= -1

    # Convert to arrays
    for key in ('p', 'v', 'Omega', 'e_R', 'tau', 'R', 'R_ref'):
        log[key] = np.array(log[key])
    log['t']   = np.array(log['t'])
    log['f']   = np.array(log['f'])

    return log


def compute_metrics(log):
    """Extract per-manoeuvre and global performance metrics."""
    t       = log['t']
    e_R_mag = np.linalg.norm(log['e_R'], axis=1)
    Omega_mag = np.linalg.norm(log['Omega'], axis=1)

    metrics = {
        'rms_eR_global': float(np.sqrt(np.mean(e_R_mag**2))),
        'peak_Omega':    float(Omega_mag.max()),
        'peak_f':        float(log['f'].max()),
    }

    # Geodesic completion error at end of each true manoeuvre segment
    manoeuvre_segs = ['backflip', 'barrel_roll', 'immelmann_roll', 'inverted_hold']
    for seg in manoeuvre_segs:
        mask = np.array(log['seg']) == seg
        if mask.any():
            last_idx = np.where(mask)[0][-1]
            R_now    = log['R'][last_idx]
            R_ref_f  = log['R_ref'][last_idx]
            trace_val = np.clip((np.trace(R_ref_f.T @ R_now) - 1) / 2, -1, 1)
            delta_deg = np.degrees(np.arccos(trace_val))
            rms_seg   = float(np.sqrt(np.mean(e_R_mag[mask]**2)))
            metrics[f'{seg}_delta_deg'] = delta_deg
            metrics[f'{seg}_rms_eR']    = rms_seg

    return metrics

def animate_acrobatics(log, out_path='outputs/05_special_entertainment/'
                       's091_acrobatics/s091_animation.gif'):
    """Animate the drone attitude and position through the acrobatic sequence."""
    import os
    os.makedirs(os.path.dirname(out_path), exist_ok=True)

    p_arr = log['p']          # (N, 3)
    R_arr = log['R']          # (N, 3, 3)
    t_arr = log['t']
    seg_arr = np.array(log['seg'])
    n_frames_total = len(p_arr)
    step = max(1, n_frames_total // 200)

    fig = plt.figure(figsize=(9, 7))
    ax  = fig.add_subplot(111, projection='3d')

    AXIS_LEN = 0.4   # body-axis arrow length

    def init():
        ax.clear()
        ax.set_xlim(-2, 2)
        ax.set_ylim(-2, 2)
        ax.set_zlim(Z_HOME - 3, Z_HOME + 3)
        ax.set_xlabel('X (m)')
        ax.set_ylabel('Y (m)')
        ax.set_zlabel('Z (m)')
        return []

    quivers = []

    def update(frame):
        nonlocal quivers
        for q in quivers:
            q.remove()
        quivers = []

        k = frame * step
        pos = p_arr[k]
        R   = R_arr[k]

        # Drone body axes
        colors_axes = ['red', 'green', 'blue']   # x, y, z body axes
        labels_axes = ['body-x', 'body-y', 'body-z (thrust)']
        for i in range(3):
            q = ax.quiver(*pos, *R[:, i] * AXIS_LEN,
                          color=colors_axes[i], lw=2)
            quivers.append(q)

        # Trail
        trail_start = max(0, k - 300)
        trail = ax.plot(p_arr[trail_start:k, 0],
                        p_arr[trail_start:k, 1],
                        p_arr[trail_start:k, 2],
                        'gray', lw=1, alpha=0.5)[0]
        quivers.append(trail)

        # Home marker (static)
        home_dot = ax.scatter(*HOME_POS, c='green', s=80,
                              marker='^', zorder=7)
        quivers.append(home_dot)

        seg = seg_arr[k]
        ax.set_title(f'S091 Acrobatics  |  t = {t_arr[k]:.2f}s  |  {seg}',
                     fontsize=10)
        return quivers

    ani = FuncAnimation(fig, update, frames=n_frames_total // step,
                        init_func=init, blit=False, interval=50)
    ani.save(out_path, writer='pillow', fps=20)
    plt.close()
    print(f'Animation saved to {out_path}')
```

## Extensions

1. **Motor saturation and torque budget**: add realistic rotor thrust limits ($\omega_{max}$) to the
   allocation matrix; compute the minimum-norm solution when the requested wrench exceeds the
   feasible polytope; plot the torque utilisation fraction $\|\tau\|/\|\tau_{max}\|$ per axis
   during each flip.
2. **Minimum-snap trajectory optimisation for attitude**: replace slerp with a minimum-snap angular
   velocity profile (Mellinger & Kumar 2011) so that angular jerk $\dot{\Omega}$ is minimised;
   compare peak torque demand and oscillations against the cubic slerp baseline.
3. **Cascaded model-predictive control (NMPC)**: replace the geometric controller with a
   nonlinear MPC horizon of 0.3 s; solve the $SO(3)$-constrained OCP in real time using the
   exponential coordinate parameterisation; compare computational cost with the analytic controller.
4. **Disturbance rejection during flips**: add Gaussian wind gusts ($\sigma = 2$ m/s) that occur
   randomly during each manoeuvre; augment the geometric controller with a disturbance observer
   (DOB) that estimates aerodynamic drag torques from the $\Omega$ residual; evaluate degradation of
   $\delta_k$ with and without the DOB under different gust intensities.
5. **Multi-drone synchronised aerobatics**: extend to three drones executing the same sequence in
   formation with $\pm 2$ m lateral offset; add a formation-keeping term to the position controller
   ($K_{form}$) that penalises deviation from the formation centroid; visualise the choreography
   with simultaneous body-axis arrows for all three vehicles.
6. **Hardware-in-the-loop with Crazyflie**: export the geometric controller gains to the Crazyflie
   firmware (C implementation of the vee map and $SO(3)$ error); replay the attitude reference via
   MAVLink reference waypoints; compare simulated vs measured $e_R$ to identify unmodelled
   aerodynamic effects near stall.
