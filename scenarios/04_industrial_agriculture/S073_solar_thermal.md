**Domain**: Industrial & Agriculture | **Difficulty**: ⭐ | **Status**: `[ ]` Not Started
**Algorithm**: Lawnmower Sweep + Thermal Anomaly Detection | **Dimension**: 2D

---

## Problem Definition

**Setup**: A single drone inspects a $80 \times 60$ m solar farm containing 120 panels arranged in
10 rows of 12. The drone carries a nadir-pointing thermal camera and flies a **constant-velocity
boustrophedon (lawnmower)** pattern at altitude $h = 5$ m and speed $v = 1.0$ m/s with a 20%
cross-track strip overlap. The thermal camera captures a swath of width
$w_{foot} = 2h\tan(\theta_{FOV}/2)$ below the drone.

A simulated thermal map is generated by placing Gaussian hot spots at $M = 8$ randomly selected
defective panels, each elevated 10–20°C above the nominal panel temperature. A panel is
**detected** when the drone's sensor footprint passes over it and the observed temperature
exceeds the detection threshold. Motion blur at higher speeds degrades image quality: the blur
radius grows linearly with speed, and detection is suppressed when blur exceeds a maximum
tolerance $b_{max}$. The nominal inspection speed $v = 1.0$ m/s is well below the critical
limit $v_{max} = 1.5$ m/s.

**Roles**:
- **Drone**: single UAV executing the pre-planned lawnmower path at constant speed $v = 1.0$ m/s;
  no in-flight replanning.
- **Solar farm**: $80 \times 60$ m rectangular array of $10 \times 12$ panels; $M = 8$ panels
  have randomly assigned thermal defects (hot spots).
- **Thermal camera**: nadir-pointing sensor with FOV $\theta_{FOV} = 45°$; produces a continuous
  thermal map sampled at each simulation timestep.

**Objective**: Fly the complete lawnmower inspection path and report:

1. **Detection rate** $R_{det}$ — fraction of the $M = 8$ defective panels correctly identified.
2. **False alarm count** $N_{FA}$ — healthy panels flagged above the detection threshold (due to
   sensor noise).
3. **Coverage time** $T_{cov}$ (s) — total mission time to scan the full farm.
4. **False negative rate** $P_{FN}$ — probability of missing a defective panel due to motion blur.

---

## Mathematical Model

### Thermal Sensor Footprint and Strip Geometry

The drone flies at altitude $h$ with a thermal camera whose horizontal field of view is
$\theta_{FOV}$. The ground footprint width (cross-track swath) is:

$$w_{foot} = 2h \tan\!\left(\frac{\theta_{FOV}}{2}\right)$$

At $h = 5$ m and $\theta_{FOV} = 45°$:

$$w_{foot} = 2 \times 5 \times \tan(22.5°) \approx 4.14 \text{ m}$$

Given a cross-track overlap ratio $\rho = 0.20$, the optimal strip spacing is:

$$d = w_{foot} \cdot (1 - \rho)$$

The number of strips required to cover the $W = 60$ m cross-track width is:

$$N_{strips} = \left\lceil \frac{W}{d} \right\rceil$$

### Waypoint Generation (Boustrophedon)

Strip $i$ ($i = 0, 1, \ldots, N_{strips}-1$) is centred at cross-track position:

$$y_i = \left(i + \tfrac{1}{2}\right) d$$

The along-track start and end of strip $i$ alternate direction:

$$\mathbf{w}_{i}^{start} = \begin{cases} (0,\; y_i) & i \text{ even} \\ (L,\; y_i) & i \text{ odd} \end{cases}, \qquad \mathbf{w}_{i}^{end} = \begin{cases} (L,\; y_i) & i \text{ even} \\ (0,\; y_i) & i \text{ odd} \end{cases}$$

where $L = 80$ m is the along-track farm length. Total path length is:

$$L_{total} = N_{strips} \cdot L + (N_{strips} - 1) \cdot d$$

### Thermal Map and Hot Spot Model

The simulated thermal map $T(x, y)$ is the sum of the nominal background temperature and
Gaussian hot spots centred on each defective panel:

$$T(x, y) = T_{nominal} + \sum_{j=1}^{M} \Delta T_j \exp\!\left(-\frac{(x - x_j)^2 + (y - y_j)^2}{2\sigma_{hot}^2}\right)$$

where:

- $T_{nominal} = 35°\text{C}$ — nominal panel operating temperature
- $\Delta T_j \sim \mathcal{U}(10, 20)°\text{C}$ — hot spot excess temperature for panel $j$
- $(x_j, y_j)$ — centre of defective panel $j$
- $\sigma_{hot} = 0.4$ m — Gaussian spread of hot spot (one panel half-width)

### Detection Condition

At each simulation timestep the drone is at position $(x_d, y_d)$. A panel centred at
$(x_p, y_p)$ is considered **within the sensor footprint** if:

$$|x_d - x_p| \leq \frac{L_{panel}}{2} \quad \text{and} \quad |y_d - y_p| \leq \frac{w_{foot}}{2}$$

where $L_{panel}$ is the along-track panel length. Detection is triggered when both the
temperature condition and the blur condition are satisfied simultaneously:

$$\text{detected} \iff T(x_p, y_p) > T_{nominal} + \Delta T_{thresh} \quad \text{AND} \quad b < b_{max}$$

### Motion Blur Model

The blur radius (in pixels) is proportional to the product of drone speed and camera exposure
time, normalised by the Ground Sample Distance (GSD):

$$b = \frac{v \cdot t_{exp}}{\text{GSD}}$$

where $\text{GSD} = h \cdot s_w / (n_{px} \cdot f)$ is the ground sample distance per pixel.
Detection is suppressed when $b \geq b_{max}$:

$$\text{pass blur check} \iff b < b_{max} \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad v < v_{max}$$

where the maximum permissible speed is:

$$v_{max} = \frac{b_{max} \cdot \text{GSD}}{t_{exp}}$$

### False Negative Rate

The probability of a missed detection due to motion blur, assuming $v$ is the only stochastic
variable, is:

$$P_{FN} = P\!\left(b \geq b_{max}\right) = P\!\left(v \geq \frac{b_{max} \cdot \text{GSD}}{t_{exp}}\right) = P(v \geq v_{max})$$

At the nominal constant speed $v = 1.0$ m/s $ < v_{max} = 1.5$ m/s, no blur-induced false
negatives occur. For a Monte Carlo speed perturbation $v \sim \mathcal{N}(v_0, \sigma_v^2)$
with $\sigma_v = 0.1$ m/s:

$$P_{FN} = 1 - \Phi\!\left(\frac{v_{max} - v_0}{\sigma_v}\right)$$

where $\Phi$ is the standard normal CDF.

## Implementation

```python
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import matplotlib.patches as patches
import matplotlib.animation as animation

# ── Domain constants ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FARM_L          = 80.0      # m — along-track (x) length of solar farm
FARM_W          = 60.0      # m — cross-track (y) width of solar farm
N_ROWS          = 10        # number of panel rows (cross-track)
N_COLS          = 12        # number of panel columns (along-track)
N_PANELS        = N_ROWS * N_COLS   # 120 panels total
M_DEFECTIVE     = 8         # number of defective (hot) panels

# ── Drone / sensor parameters ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
ALTITUDE        = 5.0       # m — constant flight altitude
FOV_DEG         = 45.0      # deg — thermal camera horizontal FOV
OVERLAP_RATIO   = 0.20      # — cross-track strip overlap
INSPECTION_SPEED = 1.0      # m/s — nominal scan speed
GPS_ERROR       = 0.1       # m — GPS position noise std dev
DT              = 0.1       # s — simulation timestep

# ── Thermal parameters ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
T_NOMINAL       = 35.0      # °C — healthy panel temperature
DT_HOT_MIN      = 10.0      # °C — minimum hot spot excess
DT_HOT_MAX      = 20.0      # °C — maximum hot spot excess
DT_THRESH       = 8.0       # °C — detection threshold above nominal
SIGMA_HOT       = 0.4       # m — Gaussian hot spot spread
T_NOISE_STD     = 0.5       # °C — sensor thermal noise

# ── Motion blur parameters ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
T_EXPOSURE      = 0.033     # s — camera exposure time (30 fps)
GSD             = 0.02      # m/pixel — ground sample distance at h=5 m
BLUR_MAX        = 2.5       # pixels — maximum tolerable blur radius
V_MAX           = BLUR_MAX * GSD / T_EXPOSURE  # ≈ 1.5 m/s critical speed

# ── Derived geometry ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOV_RAD         = np.deg2rad(FOV_DEG)
W_FOOT          = 2.0 * ALTITUDE * np.tan(FOV_RAD / 2.0)   # swath width (m)
STRIP_D         = W_FOOT * (1.0 - OVERLAP_RATIO)            # strip spacing (m)
N_STRIPS        = int(np.ceil(FARM_W / STRIP_D))            # number of strips

# Panel geometry
PANEL_L         = FARM_L / N_COLS       # along-track panel length (m)
PANEL_W         = FARM_W / N_ROWS       # cross-track panel width (m)


def build_panel_grid():
    """Return (N_PANELS, 2) array of panel centre (x, y) positions."""
    centres = []
    for row in range(N_ROWS):
        for col in range(N_COLS):
            cx = (col + 0.5) * PANEL_L
            cy = (row + 0.5) * PANEL_W
            centres.append([cx, cy])
    return np.array(centres)


def assign_defects(panel_centres, m_defective, seed=42):
    """Randomly select m_defective panels and assign hot spot temperatures."""
    rng = np.random.default_rng(seed)
    defect_idx = rng.choice(len(panel_centres), size=m_defective, replace=False)
    delta_T = rng.uniform(DT_HOT_MIN, DT_HOT_MAX, size=m_defective)
    return defect_idx, delta_T


def build_thermal_map(panel_centres, defect_idx, delta_T,
                      nx=800, ny=600):
    """
    Build a (ny, nx) thermal image over the farm area.
    Each defective panel contributes a Gaussian hot spot.
    """
    xs = np.linspace(0, FARM_L, nx)
    ys = np.linspace(0, FARM_W, ny)
    XX, YY = np.meshgrid(xs, ys)
    T_map = np.full((ny, nx), T_NOMINAL)

    for k, pidx in enumerate(defect_idx):
        px, py = panel_centres[pidx]
        hot = delta_T[k] * np.exp(
            -((XX - px)**2 + (YY - py)**2) / (2 * SIGMA_HOT**2)
        )
        T_map += hot

    return T_map, xs, ys


def generate_lawnmower_waypoints():
    """Return ordered (x, y) waypoints for the boustrophedon thermal scan."""
    waypoints = []
    for i in range(N_STRIPS):
        y_centre = (i + 0.5) * STRIP_D
        if i % 2 == 0:
            waypoints.append((0.0,    y_centre))
            waypoints.append((FARM_L, y_centre))
        else:
            waypoints.append((FARM_L, y_centre))
            waypoints.append((0.0,    y_centre))
    return waypoints


def blur_radius(speed):
    """Compute motion blur radius in pixels for a given drone speed."""
    return speed * T_EXPOSURE / GSD


def simulate_inspection(waypoints, panel_centres, defect_idx, rng):
    """
    Simulate the thermal inspection flight.
    Returns trajectory, per-panel detection flags, and false alarm flags.
    """
    n_panels    = len(panel_centres)
    detected    = np.zeros(n_panels, dtype=bool)   # hot panels found
    false_alarm = np.zeros(n_panels, dtype=bool)   # healthy panels flagged
    trajectory  = []

    pos = np.array(waypoints[0], dtype=float)
    trajectory.append(pos.copy())

    for wp in waypoints[1:]:
        target = np.array(wp, dtype=float)
        diff   = target - pos
        dist   = np.linalg.norm(diff)
        if dist < 1e-6:
            continue
        unit   = diff / dist
        steps  = max(1, int(np.ceil(dist / (INSPECTION_SPEED * DT))))
        step_dist = dist / steps

        for _ in range(steps):
            pos = pos + unit * step_dist
            # Add GPS noise
            pos_noisy = pos + rng.normal(0, GPS_ERROR, size=2)
            trajectory.append(pos.copy())

            b = blur_radius(INSPECTION_SPEED)
            if b >= BLUR_MAX:
                continue   # blur too high — skip detection this step

            # Check each panel
            for pidx, (px, py) in enumerate(panel_centres):
                in_x = abs(pos_noisy[0] - px) <= PANEL_L / 2.0
                in_y = abs(pos_noisy[1] - py) <= W_FOOT / 2.0
                if not (in_x and in_y):
                    continue

                # Simulate measured temperature with sensor noise
                is_defect = pidx in defect_idx
                if is_defect:
                    k_def = np.where(defect_idx == pidx)[0][0]
                    base_T = T_NOMINAL  # panel peak derived from map
                else:
                    base_T = T_NOMINAL
                T_measured = base_T + rng.normal(0, T_NOISE_STD)

                # Hotspot lookup: add excess for defective panels
                if is_defect:
                    k_def = int(np.where(defect_idx == pidx)[0][0])
                    # Use peak hot spot temperature at panel centre
                    T_measured += (
                        delta_T[k_def]    # injected above; need access via closure
                        * np.exp(-GPS_ERROR**2 / (2 * SIGMA_HOT**2))
                    )

                if T_measured > T_NOMINAL + DT_THRESH:
                    if is_defect:
                        detected[pidx] = True
                    else:
                        false_alarm[pidx] = True

    return np.array(trajectory), detected, false_alarm


def compute_metrics(detected, false_alarm, defect_idx, t_mission):
    """Compute and print inspection quality metrics."""
    true_positives  = int(np.sum(detected[defect_idx]))
    detection_rate  = true_positives / M_DEFECTIVE
    false_alarms    = int(np.sum(false_alarm))
    false_neg_count = M_DEFECTIVE - true_positives
    b_nom           = blur_radius(INSPECTION_SPEED)

    print(f"Thermal camera footprint  : {W_FOOT:.2f} m")
    print(f"Strip spacing d           : {STRIP_D:.2f} m")
    print(f"Number of strips          : {N_STRIPS}")
    print(f"Total path length         : {INSPECTION_SPEED * t_mission:.1f} m")
    print(f"Coverage time             : {t_mission:.1f} s  ({t_mission/60:.2f} min)")
    print(f"Blur radius (v=1.0 m/s)   : {b_nom:.2f} px  (limit {BLUR_MAX} px)")
    print(f"Defective panels detected : {true_positives}/{M_DEFECTIVE}")
    print(f"Detection rate            : {detection_rate*100:.1f}%")
    print(f"False alarms              : {false_alarms}")
    print(f"False negatives           : {false_neg_count}")

    return detection_rate, false_alarms, false_neg_count


def run_simulation():
    rng = np.random.default_rng(0)

    panel_centres           = build_panel_grid()
    defect_idx, delta_T_arr = assign_defects(panel_centres, M_DEFECTIVE)
    T_map, xs, ys           = build_thermal_map(panel_centres, defect_idx, delta_T_arr)
    waypoints               = generate_lawnmower_waypoints()

    # Expose delta_T inside simulate_inspection via a wrapper
    n_panels = len(panel_centres)

    # Inline simulation with delta_T available
    detected    = np.zeros(n_panels, dtype=bool)
    false_alarm = np.zeros(n_panels, dtype=bool)
    trajectory  = []

    pos = np.array(waypoints[0], dtype=float)
    trajectory.append(pos.copy())
    t_mission = 0.0

    for wp in waypoints[1:]:
        target    = np.array(wp, dtype=float)
        diff      = target - pos
        dist      = np.linalg.norm(diff)
        if dist < 1e-6:
            continue
        unit      = diff / dist
        steps     = max(1, int(np.ceil(dist / (INSPECTION_SPEED * DT))))
        step_dist = dist / steps

        for _ in range(steps):
            pos        = pos + unit * step_dist
            t_mission += DT
            pos_noisy  = pos + rng.normal(0, GPS_ERROR, size=2)
            trajectory.append(pos.copy())

            b = blur_radius(INSPECTION_SPEED)
            if b >= BLUR_MAX:
                continue

            for k_idx, pidx in enumerate(range(n_panels)):
                px, py   = panel_centres[pidx]
                in_x     = abs(pos_noisy[0] - px) <= PANEL_L / 2.0
                in_y     = abs(pos_noisy[1] - py) <= W_FOOT / 2.0
                if not (in_x and in_y):
                    continue

                is_def   = pidx in defect_idx
                T_meas   = T_NOMINAL + rng.normal(0, T_NOISE_STD)
                if is_def:
                    k_def  = int(np.where(defect_idx == pidx)[0][0])
                    T_meas += delta_T_arr[k_def] * np.exp(
                        -(GPS_ERROR**2) / (2 * SIGMA_HOT**2)
                    )

                if T_meas > T_NOMINAL + DT_THRESH:
                    if is_def:
                        detected[pidx] = True
                    else:
                        false_alarm[pidx] = True

    trajectory = np.array(trajectory)

    det_rate, n_fa, n_fn = compute_metrics(
        detected, false_alarm, defect_idx, t_mission
    )

    return {
        "panel_centres": panel_centres,
        "defect_idx":    defect_idx,
        "delta_T":       delta_T_arr,
        "T_map":         T_map,
        "xs":            xs,
        "ys":            ys,
        "waypoints":     waypoints,
        "trajectory":    trajectory,
        "detected":      detected,
        "false_alarm":   false_alarm,
        "detection_rate": det_rate,
        "false_alarms":  n_fa,
        "false_negatives": n_fn,
        "t_mission":     t_mission,
    }
```

## Extensions

1. **Altitude optimisation**: sweep $h \in [3, 15]$ m and plot the joint trade-off between
   footprint width (fewer strips, faster survey) and GSD (higher resolution, finer hot spot
   localisation); identify the Pareto-optimal altitude minimising coverage time subject to
   $\text{GSD} \leq 3$ cm/pixel.
2. **Adaptive re-scan**: after the first lawnmower pass, plan a second targeted pass over all
   candidate hot panels (temperature within 3°C of the threshold) at half speed and lower
   altitude to confirm detections; measure the reduction in false negative rate.
3. **Wind-induced speed fluctuations**: model $v(t) = v_0 + \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_v^2)$ with
   $\sigma_v = 0.1$ m/s; compute $P_{FN}$ analytically using the normal CDF and compare with
   Monte Carlo results over 500 trials.
4. **Multi-drone parallel coverage**: assign non-overlapping row bands to $K$ drones; each drone
   executes its own sub-lawnmower at $v = 1.0$ m/s; compare total mission time against
   $K = 1, 2, 4$ and measure whether detection rate is maintained across all drones.
5. **Panel degradation model**: assign each panel a random degradation level $\delta_i \sim
   \mathcal{U}(0, 1)$ and model $\Delta T_i = \delta_i \cdot 20°\text{C}$; plot the detection
   rate as a function of $\Delta T_{thresh}$ and find the operating point that maximises the
   $F_1$ score (harmonic mean of precision and recall).
6. **3D terrain and panel tilt**: extend to panels tilted at angle $\alpha = 20°$ facing south;
   model the effective footprint projection as an ellipse with semi-axes dependent on tilt and
   altitude; re-derive the strip spacing condition for full coverage.
